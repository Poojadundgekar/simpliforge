SUSTAINABILITY
“Sustainable development is development that meets the needs of the present without compromising the ability of future generations to meet their own needs.”
- UN World Commission on Environment and Development
A founding value at Simpliforge is ensuring sustainability for the world. Working with additive manufacturing technologies that ‘consume only that which is essential’, Simpliforge has aggressively reduced its carbon footprint by 49% as of 2020.
OUR WORK
“SUSTAINABLE FAITH”
Although Additive Manufacturing is a near-zero waste manufacturing technology, human error, design flaws and equipment efficiency do generate some plastic waste. In collaboration with local artists, Simpliforge created multimedia paintings from this waste, upcycling it into aesthetic and artistic pieces.
Tirthankar
Here is an example of an elaborate art piece depicting the 23rd Tirthankar in Shwetambar Jain and first of his 108 Avatar ‘Shri Shankeshwar Parshwanath Bhagwan’
“Bleedfire”
A depiction of the burning bushfires in Australia that scarred the flora and fauna of the little continent, accentuated by the silent bleeding of the embers, showing just how catastrophically nature is impacted by us unassuming humans.
ADDITIVE MANUFACTURING AS A SUSTAINABLE TECHNOLOGY
SUPPORTS LOW VOLUME MANUFACTURING
Additive manufacturing supports low volume manufacturing, enabling manufacturers and companies to sustainably manufacture and scale up.
ALLOWS DECENTRALIZED MANUFACTURING
Additive Manufacturing allows decentralized manufacturing, thereby reducing the emissions supporting traditional supply chains.
ENABLES UNCONSTRAINED DESIGN MANUFACTURING
Additive Manufacturing enables unconstrained design manufacturing, reducing number of parts and consequently corresponding number of manufacturing assembly lines.
